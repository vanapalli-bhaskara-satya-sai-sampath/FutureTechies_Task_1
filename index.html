Overview

What is AQI?

Definition

The Air Quality Index (AQI) is a standardised scale used by governments and environmental agencies to communicate daily air quality to the public. In India, it is computed by the Central Pollution Control Board (CPCB) based on the concentrations of eight major pollutants.

How it is Calculated

CPCB measures concentrations of PM2.5, PM10, NO‚ÇÇ, SO‚ÇÇ, CO, O‚ÇÉ, NH‚ÇÉ and Pb at monitoring stations. Each pollutant is converted to a sub-index on a 0‚Äì500 scale. The highest sub-index value among all pollutants becomes the overall AQI.

Why it Matters for India

India is home to several of the world's most polluted cities. According to IQAir, 39 of the 40 most polluted cities globally are in South Asia ‚Äî with India accounting for the majority. Poor air quality claims an estimated 1.6 million lives in India every year.

India's AQI Standard

India uses the National AQI (NAQI) standard launched in 2014 by MoEF&CC. It uses the same 0‚Äì500 numerical scale divided into six colour-coded categories, each linked to specific health advisories for the general public and sensitive groups.

Level-by-Level Effects

Good

Pollutants

Key Air Pollutants in India

PM2.5

Fine Particulate Matter

Particles <2.5 microns penetrate deep into the lungs and enter the bloodstream. Major source in India: vehicle exhaust, crop burning, construction dust, and coal combustion.

PM10

Coarse Particulate Matter

Particles <10 microns cause irritation in the nose and throat. Sources include road dust, unpaved roads, and agricultural operations widespread across India.

NO‚ÇÇ

Nitrogen Dioxide

Emitted from vehicle engines and thermal power plants. Can irritate airways and aggravate asthma. A key contributor to smog formation in Delhi and Mumbai.

SO‚ÇÇ

Sulphur Dioxide

Released by coal burning in power plants and industries. Causes respiratory irritation and combines with water to form acid rain, damaging crops and buildings.

CO

Carbon Monoxide

A colourless, odourless gas from incomplete combustion. High concentrations reduce the oxygen-carrying capacity of blood. Traffic congestion is a primary urban source.

O‚ÇÉ

Ground-Level Ozone

Forms when sunlight reacts with NO‚Çì and VOCs. Unlike stratospheric ozone, ground-level ozone causes chest pain, coughing and throat irritation. Peaks on sunny afternoons.

Protection

How to Protect Yourself

üèÉ Outdoor Activity

  • Check AQI before any outdoor exercise.
  • Avoid outdoor activity when AQI exceeds 200.
  • Exercise in the early morning when traffic is low.
  • Stay away from heavily congested roads and construction zones.
  • Use N95 or N99 masks on high-pollution days.

üè† Indoor Air Quality

  • Use HEPA air purifiers, especially in bedrooms.
  • Keep windows closed on poor air quality days.
  • Grow indoor plants that absorb pollutants.
  • Avoid incense, agarbatti, and candles in enclosed spaces.
  • Ensure kitchen exhaust fans are working properly.

üë®‚Äçüë©‚Äçüëß Sensitive Groups

  • Children, elderly, and pregnant women should take extra caution.
  • People with asthma or COPD should carry inhalers at all times.
  • Consult a doctor for personalised air quality action plans.
  • Ensure children wear masks during commute on smoggy days.

üå± Reducing Pollution

  • Use public transport, cycle, or carpool to reduce vehicle emissions.
  • Avoid burning waste, leaves, or crop residue.
  • Switch to cleaner cooking fuels (LPG/electric vs. biomass).
  • Support anti-pollution policies and green urban initiatives.
  • Plant trees and support urban greening projects.
